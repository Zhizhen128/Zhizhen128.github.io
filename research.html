Research

I am interested in statistical procedures that remain valid after data-dependent choices: selection, adaptation, privacy-preserving mechanisms, and computational approximations.

Multiple Testing and Closed Testing Procedures

With Prof. Zijun Gao · Ongoing research, USC

This project develops and evaluates multiple-testing procedures based on the closure principle. The work benchmarks proposed procedures against Cauchy-based closed-testing baselines across structured examples such as platform-trial timelines, factorial designs, and subgroup-testing settings, with a focus on power and Type I error control.

Post-selection Inference under Differential Privacy

Early-stage research project

This project studies how selective inference changes when the selection stage is subject to differential privacy constraints. The central question is whether valid confidence intervals and uncertainty statements can be constructed when the selected index and its uncertainty are affected by privacy noise.

Adaptive E-values and Power in Structured Testing

Ongoing methodological exploration

This project investigates adaptive choices of e-value constructions in structured testing problems. The goal is to understand when covariate-guided choices can improve power while preserving valid error control.

Dynamic Portfolio Allocation and Statistical Learning

Earlier project / computational statistics

This line of work studies portfolio allocation under uncertainty using dynamic decision models and empirical backtesting. Earlier work used Markov decision processes to formalize portfolio and consumption choices.

Valuation of AI Companies using LLMs and Deep Learning

Earlier research project

This project developed a hybrid valuation framework combining deep-learning feature extraction with large-language-model signals and multi-source indicators of technological activity.
