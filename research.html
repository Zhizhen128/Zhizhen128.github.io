Research

My research interests center on reliable statistical inference in modern data-analysis workflows, especially when selection, privacy, adaptivity, or computation affects the interpretation of evidence.

Post-selection inference and differential privacy

Ongoing research project

This project studies how valid inference after model or hypothesis selection interacts with privacy-preserving data analysis. The central question is whether uncertainty introduced by selection and privacy noise can be handled in a unified inferential framework rather than treated as separate technical constraints.

Adaptive e-values and power simulation

Ongoing research project

This project investigates covariate-guided constructions of e-values and compares their power against existing methods through simulation. The emphasis is on identifying when adaptivity improves detection while preserving rigorous error control.

Valuation of AI Companies: Leveraging Large-Language Models and Deep Learning Approaches

Z. Chen · Proceedings of the 2024 10th International Conference on Computer Technology Applications

This work explored the use of large-language models and deep learning approaches in the valuation of AI companies.

Portfolio allocation and walk-forward backtesting

Course / applied research project

This project compares portfolio allocation strategies across crypto and traditional assets using monthly walk-forward backtesting and performance metrics such as return, volatility, drawdown, and risk-adjusted performance.
